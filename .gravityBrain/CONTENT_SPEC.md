# Content Specification

## 1. Content Mission

The site must help users understand each cat breed in a practical, trustworthy, and visually clear way. Every page should answer both curiosity-driven and decision-driven questions.

Core editorial question:
- `Would this breed fit my home, time, budget, personality, and expectations?`

## 2. Content Principles

### Accuracy First
- Do not make absolute claims about temperament.
- Use phrases such as `often`, `tends to`, `may`, `commonly`, and `many individuals`.
- Health information must be educational, not diagnostic.
- Mention that individual cats vary by genetics, socialization, environment, and care.

### Practical Over Decorative
Content should not only describe beauty. It should translate traits into owner impact.

Example:
- Weak: `The Persian has a beautiful long coat.`
- Strong: `The Persian's long coat is beautiful, but it usually requires frequent brushing and eye/face cleaning, so it is not ideal for owners who want a low-maintenance cat.`

### Bilingual But Localized
English and Korean pages should share the same structure, but Korean content should be written naturally for Korean users.

Korean content should include phrases like:
- `초보 집사에게 적합한가`
- `아파트 생활에 맞는가`
- `털 빠짐과 관리 난이도`
- `분양/입양 전 확인할 점`

### Trust and Transparency
Every breed page should include:
- image source/status note,
- health disclaimer,
- temperament disclaimer,
- methodology link.

## 3. Breed Detail Page Template

### 3.1 Hero Section
Required elements:
- Breed name in current language.
- English name and Korean name if useful.
- One-sentence positioning summary.
- Hero image.
- Image label if AI-generated.
- Top badges.

Example badges:
- `Large Breed`
- `High Grooming`
- `Affectionate`
- `Apartment Possible`
- `Not Low-Maintenance`

### 3.2 Quick Facts
Required fields:
- Origin
- Size
- Weight range
- Lifespan
- Coat type
- Grooming level
- Shedding level
- Activity level
- Vocalness
- Affection level
- Beginner friendliness
- Apartment fit
- Family fit

Score explanation:
- 1 = very low
- 2 = low
- 3 = moderate
- 4 = high
- 5 = very high

### 3.3 Short Summary
Length:
- English: 80-140 words.
- Korean: 250-450 characters.

Purpose:
- Give fast orientation.
- Mention ideal owner.
- Mention main caveat.

### 3.4 Best For / Think Twice If
Each breed page should include two decision boxes.

Best for:
- 3-5 bullet points.

Think twice if:
- 3-5 bullet points.

Example:
- **Best for**: owners who enjoy grooming, calm indoor homes, people who want an affectionate companion.
- **Think twice if**: you want a low-shedding cat, you are away all day, you dislike daily coat care.

### 3.5 Appearance
Cover:
- Body size and shape.
- Head and face features.
- Ear shape.
- Eye color tendencies.
- Coat length and texture.
- Common colors/patterns.
- Distinctive breed traits.

Important:
- This section is critical for image validation.
- Mention that colors/patterns can vary if the breed allows many variations.

### 3.6 Personality and Temperament
Cover:
- Affection style.
- Social behavior.
- Independence.
- Playfulness.
- Vocalness.
- Adaptability.
- Relationship with children/pets.

Avoid:
- `always`, `never`, `guaranteed`, `perfect for everyone`.

### 3.7 Care Needs
Cover:
- Grooming routine.
- Nail trimming.
- Dental care.
- Exercise and play.
- Environmental enrichment.
- Feeding considerations.
- Indoor/outdoor safety.

### 3.8 Health Notes
Cover known predispositions carefully.

Required disclaimer:
- English: `This section is educational and not a veterinary diagnosis. Always consult a veterinarian for health decisions.`
- Korean: `이 내용은 일반적인 정보이며 진단이 아닙니다. 건강 문제는 반드시 수의사와 상담하세요.`

### 3.9 Home Suitability
Cover:
- Apartment fit.
- Family fit.
- Senior owner fit.
- First-time owner fit.
- Multi-pet household fit.
- Time-alone tolerance.

### 3.10 Similar Breeds
Each breed should have 3-5 similar breed links.

Similarity criteria:
- appearance,
- temperament,
- care burden,
- owner fit,
- search comparison demand.

### 3.11 FAQ
Each breed page should include 5-8 FAQs.

English FAQ examples:
- `Is the Maine Coon a good family cat?`
- `Do Maine Coons shed a lot?`
- `Are Maine Coons good for apartments?`
- `How much grooming does a Maine Coon need?`

Korean FAQ examples:
- `메인쿤은 초보자가 키우기 좋은 고양이인가요?`
- `메인쿤은 털이 많이 빠지나요?`
- `메인쿤은 아파트에서 키울 수 있나요?`
- `메인쿤은 관리가 어려운 편인가요?`

## 4. Breed Index Content

### Intro Copy
Purpose:
- Explain what users can do on the page.
- Mention filters.
- Include main keyword naturally.

### Breed Card Content
Each card should include:
- image,
- breed name,
- 1-line summary,
- 3 badges,
- grooming score,
- activity score,
- beginner score.

## 5. Guide Content

Recommended initial guide articles:
- Best cat breeds for beginners.
- Best cat breeds for apartments.
- Low-shedding cat breeds.
- Most affectionate cat breeds.
- Quiet cat breeds.
- Large domestic cat breeds.
- Long-haired cat breeds and grooming reality.
- Cat breed comparison guide.

## 6. Voice and Tone

### English Tone
- Warm.
- Clear.
- Practical.
- Calm and trustworthy.

### Korean Tone
- 친절하지만 과장하지 않음.
- 초보자도 이해 가능한 표현.
- 분양 유도보다는 신중한 입양/양육 판단 강조.

## 7. Content Length Targets

### Breed Page
- English: 1,200-2,000 words per breed.
- Korean: 2,500-4,500 Korean characters per breed.

### Guide Page
- English: 1,500-2,500 words.
- Korean: 3,000-6,000 Korean characters.

### FAQ Answer
- English: 40-90 words.
- Korean: 120-250 characters.

## 8. Editorial Warnings

Avoid:
- Unsupported medical claims.
- Claiming any breed is fully hypoallergenic.
- Encouraging impulse buying.
- Presenting controversial traits as purely cute.
- Saying AI-generated images are real photos.

Use:
- `low-shedding` instead of `hypoallergenic` unless carefully explained.
- `may be suitable` instead of `is perfect`.
- `discuss with a veterinarian` for health issues.
