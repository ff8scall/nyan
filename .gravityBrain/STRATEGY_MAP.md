# Strategy Map

## North Star
Create the most useful bilingual cat breed guide for English and Korean users who are deciding which cat breed fits their lifestyle.

## Positioning
- **Category**: Bilingual pet information / cat breed education.
- **Angle**: Detailed, practical, visual, comparison-friendly.
- **Trust anchor**: Structured profiles, transparent image labeling, source-backed claims, cautious wording on health and behavior.

## Target Segments

### Segment A: First-Time Cat Owners
- **Needs**: Easy breed comparison, temperament, care difficulty, cost expectation.
- **Pain**: Overwhelmed by breed options and conflicting information.
- **Winning feature**: Beginner-friendly suitability score.

### Segment B: Korean Search Users
- **Needs**: Korean explanations for global breeds, realistic photos, 분양/입양 전 체크포인트.
- **Pain**: Korean content often shallow, copied, or fragmented.
- **Winning feature**: Korean-native pages with care reality and lifestyle fit.

### Segment C: Enthusiasts and Researchers
- **Needs**: Breed history, physical traits, health risks, standards-oriented details.
- **Pain**: Generic listicles lack depth.
- **Winning feature**: Consistent profile schema and comparison filters.

## Content Pillars

### Pillar 1: Breed Profiles
Each breed page should include:
- **Overview**: concise identity and ideal owner.
- **Appearance**: size, body, coat, colors, distinctive traits.
- **Personality**: social energy, affection, vocalness, independence.
- **Care**: grooming, exercise, enrichment, diet notes.
- **Health**: known predispositions with non-diagnostic caution.
- **Home fit**: apartment, families, seniors, multi-pet homes.
- **Quick facts**: lifespan, weight, coat, grooming, activity, vocalness.
- **FAQ**: localized search-intent questions.

### Pillar 2: Comparison Tools
- **Filters**: low shedding, child-friendly, apartment-friendly, affectionate, quiet, beginner-friendly.
- **Sorts**: grooming burden, activity level, size, lifespan.
- **Comparison cards**: 2-4 breeds side-by-side.

### Pillar 3: Decision Support
- **Quiz-lite**: lifestyle questions leading to recommended breeds.
- **Adoption checklist**: ethical decision support, not impulse-buy funnel.
- **Care starter kit**: monetizable but helpful.

### Pillar 4: Visual Trust
- **AI images**: possible, but must be clearly labeled and reviewed against breed traits.
- **Alternative**: licensed stock or Wikimedia/CC images where attribution is manageable.
- **Best approach**: hybrid. Use AI for hero/stylized visual consistency, and real licensed references for factual appearance if possible.

## Growth Loops
- **SEO loop**: breed page -> comparison internal links -> related questions -> more long-tail entries.
- **Quiz loop**: quiz result -> share/save -> email capture.
- **Content expansion loop**: breed pages -> care articles -> product guides.
- **Bilingual loop**: English article links Korean equivalent and vice versa.

## Technical Strategy
- **Recommended framework**: Next.js for SEO, i18n routing, structured data, image optimization, and future interactivity.
- **Alternative**: Astro if content-first and minimal app behavior is preferred.
- **Data model**: breed data as structured JSON/MDX frontmatter.
- **SEO assets**: sitemap, hreflang, schema.org Animal/Breadcrumb/FAQPage where suitable.
